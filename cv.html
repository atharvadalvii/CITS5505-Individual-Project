// About me

I am pursuing a Master of Information Technology at The University of Western Australia, specialising in Software Systems, after my Bachelors in Computer Applications from Amity University, Mumbai. My studies and project work explore how reliable web engineering, disciplined data handling, and thoughtful interfaces fit together in systems people rely on every week, from classroom exercises to production scale rollouts.

In industry I have contributed to immersive web experiences and data-centric products. At Makers Lab - Tech Mahindra, I interned on Union Bank of India’s “Uni-Verse” metaverse lounge—India’s first public-sector bank metaverse environment for retail and SME banking scenarios. I optimised more than 20 three-dimensional models and animations, measured rendering behaviour across browsers, and resolved numerous issues affecting WebGL performance, interaction logic, and UI responsiveness. As an assistant on the Atmanirbhar Krishi App, I supported integrations from government sources such as IMD, ISRO, and the Ministry of Agriculture, and helped shape a multilingual mobile experience that delivers soil and water insights at a usable cadence for farmers.

At Bharti Creations, I built web-based templates and automation for student and staff ID cards across educational institutions, reducing manual effort during bulk issuance and demonstrating how incremental automation changes day-to-day operations. Across these roles I track outcomes numerically—for example render-budget improvements near thirty percent or processing-time reductions near forty percent—because claims should be tied to evidence when collaborating with non-technical stakeholders.

Technically I am comfortable with Python, JavaScript, Java, SQL, HTML and CSS, Node.js and Express, PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQLite, Git, Docker on Linux, AWS, REST APIs, Three.js, and WebGL. I like pairing fundamentals with careful tooling choices, and I am mindful of internationalisation, accessibility basics, and performance because those qualities show up differently in field applications than in toy demos.

At UWA I am sharpening software architecture, automated testing habits, and ethical reasoning around data. I also reflect critically on AI-assisted development, using assistants as accelerators while verifying behaviour in browser DevTools rather than trusting snippets blindly. Colleagues describe me as calm under integration deadlines and explicit in documentation, including diagrams when state machines or data flows would otherwise stay implicit.

Career-wise I am drawn to teams that ship data-aware web products—whether in finance, agritech, spatial computing, or internal platforms—where maintainability and public trust compete for the same schedule. This portfolio project reinforces vanilla browser skills such as fetch-based loading, client-side persistence, and unobtrusive scripting, all of which remain relevant when debugging beneath frameworks or when constraints forbid heavy client dependencies.

Outside formal employment I continue structured learning through certifications in programming foundations and full-stack fundamentals, and I read MDN alongside primary documentation when APIs behave unexpectedly. I value transparent task breakdowns, reproducible environments, and commit messages that explain why a change exists as much as what it does. I also keep small personal utilities under version control so regressions are visible early. I welcome conversations with teams that combine engineering rigor with empathy for end users, because resilient software is as much a cultural habit as a technical one.
